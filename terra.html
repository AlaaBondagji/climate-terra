About the Project

Climate Terra is a platform that uses climate data to identify ideal locations for protecting endangered plants. By analyzing Essential Agriculture Variables (EAV) like soil moisture, temperature, and rainfall, we support sustainable farming and conservation efforts to preserve biodiversity.

Essential Agriculture Variables (EAV)

Essential Agriculture Variables are critical metrics used to monitor agricultural systems and ensure sustainability. They help in understanding how various factors such as soil health, crop growth, and climate conditions impact farming.

Key Categories of Essential Agriculture Variables

Climate Variables

Precipitation, Temperature, Solar Radiation

Soil Health

Nutrient Levels, Soil Moisture, Organic Matter

Crop Monitoring

Crop Growth, Yield Estimation, Pest Infestation

Water Resources

Irrigation Usage, Water Quality, Rainfall Patterns

Essential Agriculture Variables (EAV)

Variable Description Importance
Soil Moisture The amount of water present in the soil. Critical for plant growth and irrigation management.
Temperature Average temperature of the region. Affects crop growth rates and yields.
Rainfall Total precipitation in the area. Influences irrigation needs and soil moisture levels.
Humidity Amount of moisture in the air. Impacts plant transpiration and disease spread.
Soil pH Measure of acidity or alkalinity in the soil. Affects nutrient availability and crop selection.
Sunlight Amount of sunlight received by crops. Essential for photosynthesis and growth.

Impact of EAV on Crop Yields

By monitoring essential variables like soil moisture and temperature, farmers can adjust their practices to improve crop yields. For instance, adequate irrigation during drought conditions can prevent crop failure.

Recommendations for Sustainable Agriculture

  • Regular monitoring of soil health to optimize nutrient usage.
  • Using climate data to time planting and irrigation.
  • Implementing crop rotation to maintain soil fertility.
  • Adopting water-efficient irrigation techniques.

Endangered Plants and Causes of Extinction

Plant Name Cause of Extinction
Rice Climate change such as rising temperatures and flooding.
Coffee Climate change, deforestation, and plant disease spread.
Cocoa Climate change, desertification, and unsustainable agricultural practices.
Cacti Illegal trade and agricultural activities that lead to the destruction of their natural habitats.
Timber Trees Deforestation and overuse of timber.

Farming Guide and Recommendations

Here are some essential recommendations for protecting endangered plants and promoting sustainable farming:

  • Choosing the right site for agriculture.
  • Preserving biodiversity.
  • Using sustainable irrigation techniques.
  • Reducing the use of chemical pesticides.
  • Soil management.
  • Monitoring weather and climate.
  • Collaborating with environmental organizations.
  • Monitoring growth regularly.
